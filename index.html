Bio

Hello! I am Wenhao Gao (高文昊). I am a Ph.D. candidate at MIT, where I have the privilege of being advised by Connor W. Coley. My research is centered around accelerating the molecular discovery process by harnessing the power of artificial intelligence and autonomous experiments.

My research goal is to bring autonomous molecular discovery to the real world, which means a systematical methodology that can be applied to a wide range of molecular discovery problems, with little human intervention. This not only speeds up the discovery process but also allows us to scale it up. In my past endeavors, I have focused on developing AI algorithms with broader practical applicability for designing small organic molecules, expanding upon the synthetic accessibility and sample efficiency. Lately, I have been working on bridging the gap between AI planning and autonomous experiments, and exploring pre-training strategies for molecules.

My work in designing drug candidates for advancing human health is generously supported by Google PhD Fellowship and MIT-Takeda Fellowship. I also work with the MIT Climate and Sustainability Consortium where I apply my methods to design next-generation carbon-capture materials aimed at mitigating climate change and fostering sustainability.

You can find more information about my research, publications, and projects, as well as my professional and academic background below. Thank you for visiting, and feel free to reach out with questions or collaboration ideas.
